Meet the judges

J. Scott Christianson

J. Scott Christianson is an assistant teaching professor of management at the Trulaske College of Business. Prior to joining the Trulaske College of Business, Scott was a business owner with more than 21 years of experience in videoconferencing technology, project management and information technology. He is on the leadership teams for the State Technical College of Missouri and the MU Center for the Digital Globe.

Ebony Reed

As director of innovation and the RJI Futures Lab, Ebony Reed collaborates with RJI staff and the Missouri School of Journalism to research and test new platforms for journalism, as well as study new revenue models. Throughout her career Reed has lent her storytelling and business chops to The Associated Press, The Detroit News, the Boston Business Journal and many others.

Bill Turpin

Bill is president and CEO of the Missouri Innovation Center. Bill works with local entrepreneurs and University of Missouri students and faculty to start and grow technology-based companies. Over his 30-year career, Bill has financed, acquired, and sold a variety of high-tech companies. He was the founding CEO of four startups and a senior executive at many respected public companies, such as Netscape and Borland.

The Associated Press

The Associated Press is an independent, not-for-profit news cooperative. This team was tasked with exploring new revenue-generating opportunities for the AP’s data journalism talents, and the team sees exciting potential for the AP in the lobbying and advocacy world. Team members are MBA candidates David Campos and Lycus Woo, and graduate journalism students Uliana Pavlova and Grigor Olegovich Atanesian. The team’s mentors are AP Director of Product Ken Romano and Randy Smith, the Donald W. Reynolds Endowed Chair in Business Journalism.

Columbia Missourian

The Columbia Missourian is a community news organization managed by professional editors and staffed by Missouri School of Journalism students. The team designed a strategic plan to bring the Missourian out of a $300,000 budgetary shortfall and into a sustainable future. Team members are journalism graduate student Emily Rackers and MBA candidates Alexander Starkey and Shraddha Hegde. The team’s mentors are Missourian Interim Executive Editor Mike Jenner and Les Borgmeyer, local sales manager for Fox TV.

Burst

Burst technology helps media companies and brands collect user-generated content and integrate them into live streams. The team developed a set of strategies to maximize Burst's short-term success and long-term growth. Team members are journalism graduate student Meredith McGrath and MBA candidates Brian Throm, Erin Webb, Orvil Savery and Dakota Shanes. The team's mentors are Burst Marketing Associate Emma Locke and Les Borgmeyer, local sales manager for Fox TV.

Grafiti

Grafiti is a startup on a mission to democratize data. Its mobile product and upcoming web platform allow users to explore verified datasets, build and play with charts and then seamlessly share their creations. The team was tasked with identifying the next critical move for Grafiti to execute within the next few months, and with charting a course for productive longterm growth. Team members are journalism graduate students Michael Frieseke and Robert Jones, and MBA candidates Ryan Hogan and James Wilkinson. The team's mentors are Grafiti CEO Farhan Mustafa and Randy Smith, the Donald W. Reynolds Endowed Chair in Business Journalism.
